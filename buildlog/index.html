XD75 Re Build Log

  • The plate which we will mount the switches. There are 7 small round holes for the screws. The center rectangle cutout is for the reset pins.

  • A side view of the plate. It is an open case.

  • The XD75 pcb as ordered from Taobao. This is a hotswap pcb and you are looking at the bottom of the pcb.

  • A closeup of the hotswap addon and the leds.

  • The acrylic diffuser for the pcb leds. It was less frosted than expected.

  • I got Otemu purples. They are silent tactile of medium weights. Types pretty nicely.

  • Testing the switches with keycaps.

  • Adding in switches around the plate to ensure that the compression is okay.

  • The switches will click into the plate and press the pcb down.

  • The underside. Note that the fasteners are at the tips of the screws.

  • The switches will ensure the usb port is low enough for us to plug in.

  • Slot in the pcb followed by the acrylic into the plate. Add on the screws and pop on the holders at the bottom. Do not fasten the screws at all!

  • As you can see, the pcb is floating between the plate and acrylic. Let the holder be at the tip of the screws, the screws are of just nice length. When the switches come on, they will press down the plate.

  • Once the plate is compressed properly, add on all the switches.

  • Testing out the underlights. They come out strongly in front and behind but not to the sides.

  • Just another view of the switches.

  • The switches eat very closely to the screws at the side. And not all holes are for screws.

  • Activating leds!. Rmb to test your switches! They might not have plugged in properly as you cannot see it when plugging.

  • DSA Keycaps!

  • The finished product with some Overwatch caps :)

  • My keyboard layout. Layers 0 and 1 on the left, layers 2 and 3 on the right. Fn0 activates layer 2 on on press, Fn1 activates layer 3 on press. Fn11 toggles layer 1. This allows me to switch between a split layout and a normal layout by pressing Fn0 followed by Fn1.
